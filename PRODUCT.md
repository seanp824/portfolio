# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Product manager recruiters and hiring managers, opening the link from LinkedIn or a resume at their desk, midday, on a laptop. They skim dozens of candidate sites a week and decide in under 60 seconds whether to keep reading. Secondary: engineers and PMs Sean networks with.

## Product Purpose

Sean Harbison's personal portfolio. Sean is a USC senior in Computer Science + Business Administration aiming at product management roles. The site must prove, through its own craft, that he thinks like a PM: it frames projects as product case studies (problem, decisions, outcome) instead of code links, and the design itself demonstrates taste, intentionality, and attention to detail. Success: a recruiter remembers the site and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

Precise and confident with a streak of playful invention. Calm, architectural, every pixel intentional, but with interactive moments that reward curiosity (scroll-driven reveals, tactile hover states). Reference feel: igloo.inc's simplicity, depth, and restraint, without copying it. Voice: first-person, direct, zero filler.

## Anti-references

- Generic dev portfolio: skill-logo grids, "Hi, I'm X 👋" hero, uniform project cards, emoji-decorated headings.
- Anything that reads as a template (Bootstrap sections, stock icons, safe gray-on-white with one blue accent).
- Heavy WebGL agency theatrics that bury the content or slow the load.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the resume.** Every interaction choice is evidence of product judgment; if a flourish doesn't communicate care, cut it.
2. **Case studies, not card grids.** Projects are narratives with a problem, decisions, and an outcome, written for someone evaluating PM thinking.
3. **Depth through restraint.** Few elements, generous space, atmosphere from light, shadow, and motion rather than decoration.
4. **Motion clarifies.** Scroll animations guide reading order and reward progress; nothing animates just to animate.
5. **60-second skimmable.** A recruiter scrolling fast still gets name, positioning, three projects, and contact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA contrast. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support: all scroll/parallax effects degrade to static layout with simple fades or nothing. Keyboard-navigable, semantic landmarks, visible focus states.
